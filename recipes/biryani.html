Biryani

Description

An aromatic rice dish. Stir-fry the meat such that it is crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. Then stew it with condiments and long-grain rice. Sprinkle a few Padisarah petals before serving. Every grain is saturated with the aroma of fatty meat and condiments. The dish has an exquisite taste only found in Sumeru.


Ingredients (4 servings):

Marinade for chicken biryani

  • ½ kg chicken
  • 3 tbsp plain yogurt (curd or dahi)
  • 1 ¼ tbsp ginger garlic paste (1 inch ginger & 4 garlic cloves, crushed)
  • ½ to ¾ tsp salt (adjust to taste)
    • ¼ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ to 1 tsp red chilli powder (or paprika)
  • ½ to 1 tbsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

Whole spices

  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4 green cardamom
  • 6 cloves
  • 1 inch cinnamon
  • 1 star anise
  • ¾ tsp shahi jeera (caraway seeds, or substitute with cumin)
  • 1 strand mace

  • 2 cups basmati rice, aged
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 large onion, sliced thinly
  • ¼ to ½ cup mint leaves, chopped finely (abt 15 leaves)
  • 1 green chilli, chopped
  • ½ cup plain yogurt (indian curd)
  • ¼ to ½ teaspoon red chilli powder (optional)
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 3 ½ cups water (or coconut milk, add more if needed)
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp fried onions
  • 1 pinch saffron, soaked in 2 tbsp hot milk

Steps:

  1. Add yogurt, ginger garlic paste, salt , garam masala, turmeric powder, red chilli powder and lemon juice to a bowl. Mix well and taste the marinade. Add more spice and salt if needed.
  2. Make slits on chicken pieces. Add chicken pieces to marinade and mix well. Cover and set aside for 1 hour to overnight.
  3. Rinse basmati rice trice and soak in water for 30 minutes, then drain the rice.
  4. Add ghee to a pot then add whole spices. When spices begin to sizzle, add onions and fry until evenly light brown. Stir often to prevent burning of onions.
  5. Add chicken and saute until pale for 5 minutes.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat until the chicken is tender. Saute to evaporate excess moisture in the pot.
  7. Taste, and add more salt if needed. Add yogurt, half of the chopped mint leaves, chopped green chilli, red chilli powder and garam masala. Mix all ingredients in pot evenly.
  8. Layer chicken on the bottom of the pot. Spread the drained rice over the chicken.
  9. To a separate pot, add water and salt. The water should be slightly salty. Bring the water to a rolling boil.
  10. Pour 2 cups of the salted water on the sides of the cooking pot. Add the remaining water over the rice.
  11. Sprinkle the rest of the mint leaves. Add fried onions and saffron with soaked milk to the pot.
  12. Cover pot and cook over medium heat until rice is fluffy. (If rice is undercooked but the liquid in pot has been used up, add more boiling water accordingly)
  13. To serve, place rice on a plate and fluff out the rice with a fork. Add chicken pieces to each serving.
